Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist
 Secure Your UPI Transactions: Never enter your UPI PIN while receiving money. A PIN is only
 required for sending money or checking your balance; if someone asks you to enter it to "claim a
 prize," it is a scam.
 Verify UPI Global/Intent Links: Before clicking a payment link in a chat app (like WhatsApp), verify
 the recipient's VPA (Virtual Payment Address). Scammers often use names like
 "Electricity_Board_Official" to mask a personal ID.
 Enable Biometric Locks for Payment Apps: Use the app-lock feature on PhonePe, Google Pay, or
 Paytm. This adds a layer of security even if your phone is stolen while unlocked.
 Audit Your App Permissions: Frequently check which apps have access to your contacts,
 microphone, and SMS. Malicious "Instant Loan" apps often use these to harass students.
 Use "Login Approvals" for University Portals: Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your
 student email and portals to prevent unauthorized access to your academic records.
 Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Do not use free railway or cafe Wi-Fi to log into your bank account.
 Use a VPN or your mobile data (hotspot) for any sensitive transactions.
 Identify "Digital Arrest" Scams: Be aware that Indian law enforcement (CBI, ED, or Police) will never
 contact you via Skype or WhatsApp to place you under "digital arrest" or demand money to settle a
 case.
 Report Immediately: If you are a victim of any cyber fraud, report it within the "Golden Hour" (the
 first 2 hours) to increase the chances of freezing the stolen funds.
 Official Reporting Channels: Website: cybercrime.gov.in National Helpline: 1930
