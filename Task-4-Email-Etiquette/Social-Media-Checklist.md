Recommended Practices (Do's) 
• Audit Your Privacy Settings: Regularly review your account security to control 
exactly who can see your personal posts, tagged photos, and location data. 
• Build a Professional Brand: Use platforms like LinkedIn to highlight your 
Computer Science Engineering projects, certifications, and academic 
milestones. 
• Think Before You Post: Before sharing, ask yourself if the content aligns with the 
professional image you want a future employer or professor to see. 
• Engage in Meaningful Networking: Follow industry leaders and participate in 
thoughtful, respectful discussions related to technology and software 
development. 
• Verify Information Sources: Always check the credibility of a post or news 
article before sharing it to ensure you are not inadvertently spreading 
misinformation. 
Practices to Avoid (Don'ts) 
• Don't Share Sensitive Personal Data: Avoid posting your student ID, hostel 
room number, phone number, or Aadhaar details, as these can be used for 
identity theft. 
• Don't Post Content Depicting Policy Violations: Never share photos or text that 
show illegal acts, campus policy violations, or offensive language that violates 
ethics. 
• Don't Vent About Faculty or Peers: Avoid using social media to complain about 
professors or college staff, as these digital footprints are permanent and 
searchable by recruiters. 
• Don't Engage in Online Conflicts: Avoid "cyber-baiting" or aggressive 
arguments that could reflect poorly on your emotional intelligence and maturity. 
• Don't Plagiarize or Post Without Credit: Do not post code snippets, images, or 
articles as your own; always credit the original creator or source to maintain academic integrity.
